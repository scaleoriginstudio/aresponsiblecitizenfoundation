Debate & Speaking

Debating at ARC is
designed to build leaders.

We train students to think critically, communicate clearly, and persuade effectively — skills that sit at the heart of leadership and real-world impact.

Why Debating

To lead is to understand,
reason, and persuade.

ARC views debating as a foundational tool for leadership. Debating builds these capabilities early — forcing students to engage with complex ideas, analyse multiple perspectives, and communicate with clarity and conviction.

It also trains students to critically consume information and avoid shallow or unexamined thinking. Students who debate leave with the ability to think independently, the confidence to speak and lead, and the discipline to engage seriously with the world.

  • Understand the world — politics, economics, social issues, global affairs
  • Form strong, reasoned views backed by evidence
  • Persuade others to act — the core skill of every leader
  • Engage with complex ideas from multiple perspectives
The Format

Why parliamentary
debate?

Format

Think on Your Feet

With limited preparation time, students must rapidly analyse motions and construct arguments in real time — a skill that transfers to every high-pressure situation they'll face.

Structure

Rigorous & Accessible

Judged by informed peers with strong feedback emphasis. Rewards clarity, balance, and reasoning over style — making it both intellectually demanding and genuinely accessible.

Knowledge

Broad Literacy Required

Students stay informed about politics, economics, social issues, and global affairs — becoming engaged, informed citizens as a natural outcome of preparing to debate.

Our Role

We built the infrastructure.
Others followed.

When ARC began in 2016, structured school-level debating opportunities were geographically limited. ARC introduced parliamentary debating to Western India, built a scalable school-focused model, and created consistent pathways to national and international competition.

Over time, similar formats and structures have expanded across the country — increasing access for students from diverse regions. ARC is proud to have been part of that beginning.

  • Pioneered parliamentary debating in Western India — Gujarat & Rajasthan
  • Built consistent pathways to WSDC and national competitions
  • Hundreds of schools engaged since 2016
  • Alumni at top universities and intellectually demanding careers

01 — International Pathway

WSDC Selections —
Western India.

A pathway to represent India internationally.

ARC organises regional selections for the World Schools Debate Championships, providing students from Gujarat and Rajasthan a pathway to represent India internationally. This has been conducted annually since 2016, with participation from hundreds of schools.

  • ~150 students participating per year from 15+ schools
  • Pre-tournament workshops by WSDC and Australs champions
  • 25+ university-level adjudicators providing round feedback
  • 11 students qualified for National Camp in 2021
  • Nishka Mehta (Grade 7, Jamnagar) selected to Indian Development Squad

2021 Highlight: Pre-tournament workshops led by Shruti Deb (2021 Australs ESL Champion) and Tejas Subramanium (2019 WSDC Champion).

02 — Training

Intensive Debate
Workshops.

Small-group. Direct feedback. Real acceleration.

Focused training programmes where students are coached in the core skills of competitive parliamentary debate. The small-group format ensures every student receives direct, personalised feedback — not the passive learning of a lecture.

  • Argument construction — how to build a case from first principles
  • Rebuttal and engagement — how to respond to opposing arguments
  • Structured thinking — how to order and prioritise ideas under pressure
  • Critical reading and research — building the knowledge base to debate anything

03 — Online Initiative

ThinkOff — daily debate,
iterative growth.

Launched during COVID. Built for the long term.

ThinkOff was a daily online debating challenge where students responded to new motions each day, submitted written arguments anonymously, and received detailed feedback to improve iteratively. No school name. No reputation. Just the quality of the argument.

Think Off 3.0 featured 6 in-depth workshops on argumentation, rebuttal, critical reading, and research — and doubled as a fundraiser for COVID-19 second wave relief, procuring oxygen concentrators, ration kits, and medicines for underserved communities.

  • New debate motion released every day
  • Anonymous written submissions — judged purely on merit
  • Detailed feedback after every round for iterative improvement
  • Think Off 3.0 raised funds for COVID-19 relief

04 — Recurring Programme

Saturday Spars —
low stakes, high growth.

A recurring debate series where students from different schools participate in regular practice debates. Low-stakes by design — the goal is to build the confidence and consistency that competition cannot.

Each session ends with structured feedback from experienced adjudicators. Students return week after week, and the cumulative effect is transformative: communicators who are comfortable, confident, and consistent.

  • Regular practice debates between students from different schools
  • Structured feedback from experienced adjudicators after each round
  • No pressure — a space for experimentation and growth
  • Builds the consistency that one-off tournaments cannot

05 — Flagship Platform

ARC Leagues —
year-round competition.

A structured league system. Continuous growth.

ARC Leagues is a year-round, inter-school debating system designed as a round-robin league — enabling continuous competition and skill-building beyond seasonal tournaments. The 2021–22 edition selected 22 students based on their West Zone performance, organised them into 4 competing squads, and ran from November 2021 to January 2022.

  • 22 students selected based on West Zone performance
  • 4 competing squads across the league
  • Personalised mentorship from experienced university debaters
  • Regular improvement tasks across all aspects of debating
  • Prepares students simultaneously for National Selections

Think Off

A debate-led initiative that bridges intellectual engagement with real-world social impact — where discussion becomes action.

Overview

Think Off is a debate-led initiative by ARC Foundation that combines intellectual engagement with real-world social impact. Launched during a critical period — the second wave of the COVID-19 pandemic — the programme created a platform for students to participate in meaningful discussions while directly contributing to community-focused relief efforts.

ThinkOff 3.0 became a landmark edition: a national online debating competition that simultaneously functioned as a fundraiser for COVID-19 relief, procuring oxygen concentrators, ration kits, and vital medicines for underserved communities across India.

Programme Approach

Think Off was designed to move beyond conventional debate formats by linking discussion with action. Participants engaged in:

  • Structured debates on socially relevant and contemporary topics
  • Dialogue-driven awareness building around civic responsibilities
  • Contribution to community-oriented initiatives through participation
  • Online format enabling participation from students nationwide

This approach ensured that intellectual engagement translated into tangible social outcomes — proving that debate and social responsibility are not separate endeavours.

ThinkOff 3.0 — COVID Relief

ThinkOff 3.0 doubled as a fundraiser at a moment of acute national need. Funds raised through the event were used to:

  • Procure oxygen concentrators for COVID-19 patients
  • Distribute ration kits to families in need
  • Supply vital medicines to underserved communities

The initiative demonstrated how a debate platform, when thoughtfully designed, can mobilise youth energy toward immediate, measurable community impact.

Social Impact Integration

The initiative successfully bridged youth discourse and community impact by encouraging participants to actively support causes while engaging in debate. Key focus areas included:

  • Awareness generation on pressing social issues including public health and civic responsibility
  • Fundraising and support initiatives during periods of crisis
  • Encouraging responsible and informed civic participation among students

Impact Highlights

  • Integration of national online debate competition with social impact
  • Active student engagement during the COVID-19 pandemic
  • Raised funds directly channelled to COVID relief (oxygen, ration kits, medicines)
  • Increased civic awareness and community participation

Project Outcome

Think Off demonstrates how structured debate platforms can be leveraged to drive both intellectual development and social responsibility — creating a model where youth engagement directly contributes to community impact. The COVID relief fundraiser edition stands as one of ARC's most direct instances of debate translating into real-world action.

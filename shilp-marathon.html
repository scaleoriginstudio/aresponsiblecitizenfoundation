Environmental Action

Shilp Aarambh — Official Recycling Partner

12,000+ plastic bottles. 20,000 citizens. One sustainability pledge. ARC Foundation as Official Recycling Partner at the Shilp Marathon 2024.

12,000+Bottles Collected
80 kgPlastic Diverted
20,000+Citizens Engaged
40+Volunteers

Overview

ARC Foundation partnered with the Shilp Marathon for the second consecutive year as the official Recycling Partner, implementing a structured waste management and sustainability initiative at a large public event. The project focused on waste segregation, plastic recycling, volunteer mobilisation, and environmental awareness — demonstrating how high-footfall events can be converted into platforms for measurable ecological impact.

Waste Management & Recycling Impact

The initiative delivered tangible outcomes through on-ground execution and efficient waste handling systems:

  • 12,000+ plastic bottles collected across the event
  • 80 kg of plastic waste diverted from landfills
  • Recycled material repurposed into school supplies for underprivileged communities

This approach ensured that waste generated during the event was not only managed responsibly but also redirected toward social good — closing the loop between environmental action and community benefit.

Volunteer-Led Execution

The project was driven by a network of 40+ student volunteers who played a critical role in:

  • Enabling on-site waste segregation across plastic, food, and paper streams
  • Guiding participants and marathon runners toward responsible disposal practices
  • Creating awareness around sustainability and recycling at scale
  • Including and recognising sanitation workers and Swachhata Karmis as equal contributors

Their involvement ensured both operational efficiency and meaningful community engagement, with dignity and collaboration at the centre of the execution model.

Innovation — Reverse Vending Machine

A key feature of the initiative was the deployment of a Reverse Vending Machine, which processed plastic bottles into recyclable flakes in real time during the event. This demonstrated a scalable and efficient model for event-based plastic waste management, combining technology with on-ground action — and gave participants a visible, tangible experience of what recycling looks like at scale.

Large-Scale Public Participation

More than 20,000 participants collectively engaged in a sustainability pledge, committing to:

  • Reduce waste and adopt responsible consumption patterns
  • Protect and conserve natural resources
  • Promote environmentally sustainable practices in daily life

The scale of participation made this one of the largest public sustainability commitments ARC has facilitated.

Partnerships & Recognition

The initiative was conducted in collaboration with the Snehshilp Foundation, led by Snehal Brahmbatt. The event was attended by Sonu Sood, who acknowledged the importance of sustainability-driven initiatives and the power of citizen participation in environmental responsibility.

Impact Highlights

  • 12,000+ plastic bottles collected; 80 kg diverted from landfill
  • Recycled plastic repurposed into school supplies for underserved students
  • 40+ volunteers coordinated on-ground waste management systems
  • Reverse Vending Machine deployed — real-time plastic-to-flakes processing
  • 20,000+ citizens engaged in a public sustainability pledge
  • Collaboration with Snehshilp Foundation; recognition by Sonu Sood

Project Outcome

The Shilp Marathon initiative demonstrates ARC Foundation's ability to execute large-scale waste management projects, mobilise communities, and integrate sustainability into high-impact public events. It serves as a replicable model for combining environmental responsibility, technology, and citizen engagement to deliver measurable results — and proves that public events can be net-positive environmental moments rather than sources of waste.

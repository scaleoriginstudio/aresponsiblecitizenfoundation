Saturday Spars

A recurring weekly debate practice platform — where consistency, peer learning, and steady repetition quietly build the most capable communicators.

WeeklySessions
OpenAll Levels
PeerLearning Model

Overview

Saturday Spars is a recurring debate practice platform by ARC Foundation designed to build consistency in public speaking, critical thinking, and structured argumentation among students. The initiative provides a regular space for participants to engage in debates, ensuring continuous learning and improvement through practice.

Where competitions test students, Saturday Spars trains them. The weekly rhythm is the point: showing up regularly, getting feedback, adapting, improving.

Programme Structure

Saturday Spars operates as a weekly engagement platform, where students participate in:

  • Structured debate sessions on diverse and contemporary topics
  • Argument construction and rebuttal exercises
  • Moderated discussions and structured peer feedback
  • Practice rounds in British Parliamentary and other formats

The consistent format allows participants to refine their skills over time through repetition and active participation, rather than cramming for specific events.

Skill Development Focus

Through regular sessions, participants develop:

  • Strong communication and public speaking abilities
  • Improved critical thinking and reasoning skills
  • Confidence in articulating ideas clearly and effectively
  • Adaptability in responding to different perspectives and counter-arguments

Peer Learning Environment

The platform fosters a collaborative learning environment, where students learn not only through participation but also by observing and engaging with peers. This encourages:

  • Knowledge exchange between students of different experience levels
  • Constructive feedback culture where growth is the shared goal
  • Continuous improvement driven by peer accountability

Impact Highlights

  • Weekly student engagement through structured sessions
  • Consistent skill-building through practice and repetition
  • Development of a peer-driven learning environment
  • Strong pipeline of students prepared for ARC Leagues and WSDC selections

Project Outcome

Saturday Spars creates a foundation for long-term development in debate, communication, and analytical thinking — preparing students for competitive platforms and real-world discourse. The weekly commitment builds habits that carry beyond the room.

Speak Easy

A safe, low-stakes space for public speaking and creative expression — designed for students who want to find their voice before they compete.

SafeLow-Barrier Space
OpenInclusive Format
FirstSteps in Speaking

Overview

Speak Easy is a public speaking and creative expression platform by ARC Foundation that enables students to explore ideas through debate, storytelling, and open dialogue. The initiative is designed to help participants develop a clear, confident voice in a supportive and low-barrier environment — before they enter the competitive arena.

Not every student begins as a confident speaker. Speak Easy exists precisely for that moment before confidence arrives — a space where hesitation is expected, and growth is the only measure of success.

Programme Approach

Speak Easy focuses on expression over competition, providing a space where participants can:

  • Speak on diverse and contemporary topics without formal judging pressure
  • Experiment with storytelling and narrative formats alongside structured argument
  • Engage in open discussions and idea-sharing with peers
  • Receive supportive, constructive feedback from facilitators

This flexible structure encourages participants to communicate freely while refining their delivery and thought process at their own pace.

Skill Development Focus

Through participation, students build:

  • Strong public speaking and articulation skills from the ground up
  • Confidence in expressing opinions and perspectives publicly
  • Ability to structure thoughts creatively and effectively
  • Comfort with speaking in front of an audience — however small

Supportive Learning Environment

The platform emphasises a non-judgmental and inclusive environment, allowing participants to:

  • Overcome hesitation and stage fear in a low-stakes setting
  • Develop their unique communication style without external pressure
  • Learn through observation and peer interaction
  • Build the confidence needed to move toward competitive formats like Saturday Spars and ARC Leagues

Impact Highlights

  • Development of public speaking skills among first-time participants
  • Encouragement of creative expression alongside structured debate
  • Increased confidence and reduced stage fear among participants
  • Gateway programme feeding students into ARC's wider debate ecosystem

Project Outcome

Speak Easy creates an accessible entry point into communication and expression — helping students build confidence and develop skills that are essential across academic, professional, and social contexts. The programme reflects ARC's belief that everyone has something worth saying.

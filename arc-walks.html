ARC Walks & Land Reclamation

What started as community clean-up walks became a systematic waste management operation — and then something unexpected: 8 acres of degraded land brought back to life.

Overview

ARC Foundation has implemented a comprehensive waste management and land reclamation initiative that integrates community participation with long-term environmental systems. The project combines clean-up drives, village-level waste infrastructure, and innovative land restoration techniques to address waste management challenges while improving land usability and ecological conditions.

Community Clean-Up Drives — Walk with a Purpose

The initiative began with Walk with a Purpose — ARC Walks, a series of community-led clean-up drives designed to mobilise citizens and youth groups. Participants actively engaged in:

  • Collection of plastic and mixed waste from public spaces
  • Promotion of responsible disposal practices among passersby
  • Awareness-building around cleanliness, sanitation, and sustainability

These drives played a critical role in improving local sanitation conditions while fostering a culture of shared civic responsibility. The first ARC Walk took place on 1st February 2020, when twenty-five young volunteers gathered outside Silver Oak University, Gota at 7am — working alongside Retired Wing Commander Dharmendra Sharma to clear the surrounding area and collect ten full bags of waste including Vimal packets and plastic items, disposed with AMC truck support.

"As you sow, so shall you reap." — The guiding principle of every ARC Walk.

Village Waste Management — Pratapgarh & Ramnagar

Building on community engagement, ARC Foundation adopted the villages of Pratapgarh and Ramnagar to implement structured waste management systems. The intervention included:

  • Regular waste collection mechanisms established at village level
  • Segregation at source — plastic, organic, and recyclable waste streams
  • Introduction of recycling processes and community training
  • Infrastructure installed for self-sustaining waste management
  • Partnership with AMC and local municipal bodies

This system-based approach ensures long-term improvements in waste handling, sanitation, and environmental health at the village level — with community-led maintenance after ARC Foundation's exit.

Land Reclamation Through Foundry Sand

The project extends beyond waste management into land restoration, using an innovative technique: repurposing foundry sand — a byproduct of industrial casting processes — to restore erosion-prone land. This method was applied in degraded areas to:

  • Fill and stabilise degraded land that had been damaged by erosion
  • Improve soil stability and structural integrity
  • Restore land usability for community and ecological purposes

As a result, approximately 8 acres of land have been successfully reclaimed — reducing environmental degradation while creating functional land resources.

Impact Highlights

  • Community-driven clean-up operations across multiple locations
  • Active participation from youth groups and volunteers
  • Adoption of 2 villages for structured waste management (Pratapgarh, Ramnagar)
  • End-to-end waste collection, segregation, and recycling systems
  • 8 acres of land restored through foundry sand-based reclamation
  • Reduction in soil erosion and improved land usability
  • 300+ households reached through waste management programmes

Project Outcome

This initiative demonstrates a scalable model for integrated waste management in rural and semi-urban areas, combining grassroots participation with technical solutions. By linking waste management, community engagement, and land restoration, ARC Foundation has created a replicable framework for sustainable environmental development.
